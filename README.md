# 🪨 Desktop Pet Rock

*A virtual pet rock that needs minimal care, gets dusty, produces gems, and may roll away if ignored.*

---

## What is this?

It's a rock. But it has needs.

Click to pet it (makes it happy). Double-click to polish (cleans it). Right-click to give it a name. It gathers dust over time. Occasionally it burps up a gem—click the gem to collect it. Ignore it for too long and it will literally roll away. Then you have to find it.

Minimal effort pet ownership. Zero cleanup. Just a rock with feelings.

---

## Features

- **Happiness system** — petting increases happiness, neglect decreases it
- **Dust accumulation** — rock gets dusty over time; double-click to clean
- **Gem drops** — happy rocks occasionally produce gems (click to collect)
- **Roll away** — if ignored for 2+ minutes, rock may roll off-screen
- **Blinking eyes** — rock blinks randomly
- **Naming** — right-click to personalize your rock
- **Visual states** — happy (glow), dusty (sepia), dirty (gray), sad (shrunk)
- **Stats tracking** — pet count, gems collected, clean count, time together
- **Secret interactions** — try different click patterns
- **Zero maintenance** — no feeding, no walks, no vet bills

---

## How to Interact

| Action | Effect |
|--------|--------|
| **Single click** | Pet the rock (+happiness, -dust, spawn hearts) |
| **Double-click** | Polish/clean the rock (removes all dust) |
| **Right-click** | Rename your rock |
| **Click gem** | Collect it (+gem count) |
| **Ignore >2min** | Risk rolling away (find it by clicking around) |

---

## Rock States

- **Happy** — bright, slightly glowing, smiling mouth
- **Content** — neutral, normal appearance
- **Dusty** — sepia-tinted, needs cleaning soon
- **Dirty** — darker, sad expression, urgent cleaning needed
- **Sad** — grayscale, small, downturned mouth (from neglect)
- **Rolling** — currently rolling away (find it!)

---

## The Real Story

I wanted a pet that requires exactly zero effort but still gives the *illusion* of companionship. A rock is the perfect pet: it's already emotionally available. It just sits there. Occasionally it does something (gems, blink, roll) to remind you it exists. You can project whatever feelings you want onto it.

Also: rocks are low-maintenance. Unlike that emotionally-fragile button, this one just needs occasional attention and it'll stay put.
